As a teen, I tried writing lyrics. When I think about them, I'm embarrassed...not really confident that I can do any better now that I'm grown up.

So don't expect to see any actual lyrics here. For now, this will be my "notepad" for ideas.

It's Electronic Way

A play from something I often say about email; "It's winging it's electronic way"

It's a love song. The full title could be "My Love Wings It's Electronic Way"

The chorus would include that line, maybe something more.

And each verse would be about a different way of saying "I love you" to Carmella (my wife, for anybody that doesn't know).

  • Text message
  • Email
  •  ? Skype ?
  • Phone call (goes to voice mail...:)

... and a different message of "I love you" for each type, too.

This one could be fun!

Common Sense Blues

A lament on the state of the world, and the loss of common sense.

A couple of snippets 

Did I rape you because I held the door?
Well, dressed like that, you raped me more.

...and of course, the "tag line" is 

I've got the common sense blues

Why Would You Call Me That?

(or "What did I ever do to you")

chorus goes something like: 

what did I ever do to you, 
that you would call me that?

primarily, it's a song about racism, but also about stereotyping, prejudice and slander.

My Boss Don't Love Me Blues

All about working hard, being the best, but being unappreciated.

But there should be an undercurrent about not being perfect, but never explicitly stated.

This should be playable as a "working man's anthem" - but also should have honesty in it about how we tell ourselves we're better than we really are.

Untitled #1

A song about watching a good friend get totally beaten. Maybe physically, maybe emotionally...maybe both.

But the song is a metaphor for how the US is being torn down.


Your gods Have Lied to You

  • government as god
  • science as god
  • false religions
  • addictions

Promises of peace and prosperity that can't be kept Careful not to say "there is no God" - maybe even a clear statement that they are *false* gods...but let the contrast hint that there is a real God that CAN fulfill the promises.


Each verse starts with the same promise (sort of like a chorus...or like part of the chorus)...but the way the promise is failed is what defines the verse.

the bridge/refrain is a litany of false promises.
